A Brief Introduction

Helena Petrovna Blavatsky[a] (née Hahn von Rottenstern; 12 August [O.S. 31 July] 1831 – 8 May 1891), often known as Madame Blavatsky, was a Russian and American mystic and author who co-founded the Theosophical Society in 1875. She gained an international following as the leading theoretician of Theosophy. Born into an aristocratic family in Yekaterinoslav, then in the Russian Empire (now Dnipro in Ukraine), Blavatsky traveled widely around the empire as a child. Largely self-educated, she developed an interest in Western esotericism during her teenage years. According to her later claims, in 1849 she embarked on a series of world travels, visiting Europe, the Americas, and India. She also claimed that during this period she encountered a group of spiritual adepts, the "Masters of the Ancient Wisdom", who sent her to Shigatse, Tibet, where they trained her to develop a deeper understanding of the synthesis of religion, philosophy, and science.

Literature Works

  • From The Caves And Jungles Of Hindostan

  • The Secret Doctrine

    • The Secret Doctrine" is a significant book on esotericism that synthesizes science, religion, and philosophy. It was originally published in two volumes in 1888 and written by Helena Petrovna Blavatsky

  • Voice Of Silence

    • "The Voice of the Silence" is a book first published in 1889 by H.P. Blavatsky, a prominent figure in the Theosophical Society. The book emphasizes the importance of inner harmony to perceive truth, highlighting the need to transcend sensory illusions to truly see, hear, understand, and remember


  • Isis Unvieled

    • The book "Isis Unveiled" by Helena Petrovna Blavatsky is a significant work of esoteric philosophy and a critical response to materialism in science and religion. It was first published in 1877 and explores ancient and modern science and theology. Blavatsky's combination of original insights and scholarly sources in the book has been praised by modern occultism for defying materialist science 1 2 . However, the book has faced criticism for extensive plagiarism, with some scholars accusing it of borrowing extensively from other sources without proper acknowledgment 3 4 . Despite the mixed reception, "Isis Unveiled" remains a notable work in the realm of esoteric philosophy and theosophy.
